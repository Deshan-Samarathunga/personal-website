Lecture 5: CV Creation, LinkedIn, GitHub, and Career Networking

I learned that a CV should highlight relevant skills, education, projects, certifications, and achievements in a direct way. LinkedIn and GitHub also act as professional evidence, especially for IT students. I will apply this by improving my GitHub repositories, adding meaningful README files, showing backend/API/database work, and using LinkedIn to connect with industry professionals and internship opportunities.

Lecture 6: Interview Preparation and Employer Expectations

This lecture helped me see interviews from the employer perspective. Employers evaluate technical ability, problem-solving, communication, attitude, teamwork, and cultural fit. Common mistakes include poor preparation, vague answers, weak examples, and not understanding the company. I will prepare by researching employers, reviewing backend concepts, practicing common questions, and explaining how I approach problems.

Lecture 7: Non-Verbal Communication and Body Language

I learned that body language can support or weaken a spoken answer. Eye contact, posture, hand gestures, facial expression, and voice modulation all affect confidence and clarity. For virtual interviews, lighting, camera position, background, internet connection, and microphone quality also matter. I will apply this by practicing mock interviews, speaking calmly, sitting professionally, and testing my setup before online interviews.

Lecture 8: STAR Method and Mock Interview Practice

The STAR method taught me how to answer behavioral questions using Situation, Task, Action, and Result. It helps avoid unclear answers and shows real evidence of skills. I will use STAR to explain project work, teamwork, debugging, and problem-solving experiences. For example, I can describe a backend issue, my responsibility, the steps I took to fix it, and the final improvement to the project.
